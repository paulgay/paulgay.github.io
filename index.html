The story behind my resume (pdf here

) Studies: I graduated in computer sciences from INSA Rouen (France) in 2011 and obtained at the same time a M. of Sciences from university of Rouen. There I discovered machine learning, and get involved in the research projects of the LITIS laboratory, building numerical instruments with the company "La Grande Fabrique" and acquiring and processing EEG signals with Rémy Flamary. To complete this master, I had the chance to found an internship position with Ronan Fablet, in Peru, on the classification of underwater animals in acoustic data.
Phd: After this warm up, I started a phd under the supervision of Sylvain Meigner (LIUM, France) and Jean Marc Odobez (IDIAP Research Institute, Switzerland). My research there focused on unsupervised Audio-visual person identification in broadcast data. The major part of this work consisted in improving speaker diarization and face clustering systems. I used probabilistic graphical models to integrate the different modalities in a global framework. This work took place in the context of the REPERE evaluation campaign and the european project EUMSSI.
Avignon teaching period: During the school year 2014-2015, I was a teaching assistant (ATER) at Avignon university and member of the LIA lab.
Post-doc in Italy: I am currently a post-doctoral fellow at IIT/VGM (Genova Italy) with Alessio Del Bue, where I work on merging multiple view geometry and machine learning. We build models which understand a 3D scene by localizing the objects, estimate their occupancy and recognize the relations between them. I also advise the PhD student Vaibhav Bansal.
